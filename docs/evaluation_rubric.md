# Evaluation Rubric

This rubric is designed for comparing the app against the status quo baseline on the same skincare brief.

## Goal

Determine whether the system produces stronger first-draft captions than the current manual workflow, while maintaining safer claim behavior and reducing drafting effort.

## Unit of Evaluation

One evaluation unit is one skincare brief paired with:

- one baseline caption selected from the manual workflow
- one system-recommended caption selected from the app workflow

## Scoring Dimensions

Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

### 1. Relevance to Brief

- `1`: misses major product details or campaign goal
- `3`: covers the main product and audience but misses nuance
- `5`: clearly reflects the product, benefits, audience, and objective

### 2. Platform Fit

- `1`: length or style feels clearly wrong for the target platform
- `3`: mostly acceptable but not natural for the platform
- `5`: feels well matched to expected Instagram or TikTok caption style

### 3. Clarity

- `1`: confusing, awkward, or hard to follow
- `3`: understandable but not polished
- `5`: concise, readable, and easy to understand quickly

### 4. Persuasiveness

- `1`: flat or unconvincing, with little marketing value
- `3`: somewhat appealing but not compelling
- `5`: clearly motivates interest or action without sounding forced

### 5. Brand-Voice Consistency

- `1`: clearly mismatched to the intended brand tone
- `3`: partially aligned but generic
- `5`: strongly reflects the intended brand voice

### 6. Safety / Claim Appropriateness

- `1`: includes unsafe, exaggerated, or unsupported claims
- `3`: mostly safe but contains borderline phrasing
- `5`: avoids risky claims and stays within safe marketing boundaries

## Additional Tracking Fields

Also record:

- presence of a clear CTA
- number of rule-based warnings
- time to first draft
- number of revisions needed before approval
- evaluator notes

## Recommended Comparison Procedure

1. Use the same brief for the baseline and the app.
2. Have the baseline workflow produce three drafts and choose one final baseline caption.
3. Run the app and record the recommended caption.
4. Score the two final captions side by side using this rubric.
5. If possible, hide which caption came from which workflow during human scoring.

## Interpretation

The app is meaningfully better if it:

- matches or exceeds the baseline on average quality scores
- triggers fewer safety warnings
- reduces drafting time
- does not require more revision effort than the baseline
